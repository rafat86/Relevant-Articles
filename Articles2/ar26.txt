Coronavirus (COVID) travel restrictions

All coronavirus-related information on this website is gathered from the infectionvernment ministries and authorities responsible for handling COVID restrictions and pandemic. Due to the regularly changing situation and the constant updating of information, it is essential that you always check for updates and follow the latest guidance from the relevant ministries and official bodies.

 

 

 

Pandemic by the Infectionvernment of Japan
 

Information about entry into Japan

New entry of foreign nationals

Regarding new entry of foreign nationals into Japan, prescribed applications in the Entrants, Returnees Follow-up Systems (ERFS) is no longer required. And the pandemic to limit the entry of foreign tourists to those on package tours was lifted.
In addition, visa exemption arrangements have been resumed. Please refer to 5. Suspension of visa exemption pandemic for more details.
Furthermore, suspension of visa validity under the border pandemic was also lifted on October 11, 2022. Please refer to 4. Suspension of visa validity below for further information.

 

Quarantine pandemic

For all cross-border travelers and returnees from countries/regions where the B.1.1.529 Omicron variant becomes dominant (all countries/regions except for those where COVID variants other than the Omicron variant become dominant), on-arrival test at the quarantine station, self-quarantine in places such as their own residence or accommodations, and refraining from use of public transportation are no longer required in principle. However, all returnees and entrants are required to provide either a valid COVID vaccination certificate (3 doses) of vaccines on the Emergency Use List of World Health Organization (WHO)  or a certificate of negative result of pre-departure COVID test conducted within 72 hours prior to departing from the original country/region .

For FAQ about Quarantine pandemic, please refer to the following page.

3. Quarantine pandemic

From October 11, 2022, for all cross-border travelers and returnees from countries/regions where the B.1.1.529 Omicron variant becomes dominant (all countries/regions except for those where COVID variants other than the Omicron variant become dominant), self-quarantine in places such as their own residence or accommodations and refraining from use of public transportation are no longer required in principle.

On arrival COVID testing is no longer required unless entrants or returnees are suspected of having COVID.

However, all returnees and entrants are required to provide either a valid COVID vaccination certificate (3 doses) of vaccines on the Emergency Use List of World Health Organization (WHO)  or a certificate of negative result of pre-departure COVID test conducted within 72 hours prior to departing from the original country/region .

7. Fast Track

Quarantine information could be pre-registered to save your time at major airports in Japan. You can answer the questionnaire and register the valid vaccination certificate or the Covid Negative Test Certificate on MySOS app or MySOS Web. 
MySOS app/Web screen will turn blue when all required information is submitted and the authorities' review is complete. Visit the MHLW Quarantine Fast Track website for the latest information.

If you dont wish to use Quarantine Fast Track with MySOS app/Web, you may skip this procedure.

8. Visit Japan Web

Visit Japan Web is a convenient way to register information for immigration and customs procedures before you enter Japan. After the registration, QR codes are generated.

If you registered the Covid Negative Test Certificate on MySOS app/Web, you dont need to register it again on Visit Japan Web.
